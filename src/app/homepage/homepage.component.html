About Mahakaleshwar Temple

The Mahakaleshwar Temple in Ujjain, Madhya Pradesh, is one of the most revered Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. It houses one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, believed to be born of itself (Swayambhu), deriving currents of power (Shakti) from within itself.

The temple is located on the banks of the holy Shipra River and is known for its unique Bhasma Aarti, performed every morning with sacred ash. This ancient ritual attracts thousands of devotees from across the world.

Ujjain, one of the seven sacred cities (Sapta Puri) in Hinduism, has been a center of learning and spirituality for millennia. The Mahakal temple stands as a testament to India's rich spiritual heritage.
